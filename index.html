About Me

How I [became a] Quant

I presume that my strong interest in Quantitative Finance emanated from the fact that in High School I used to watch and follow documentaries and news about the Global Financial Crisis. Through these I discovered a trending profession which was at the center of most discussions; “Quant”. With much curiosity I researched more and I found myself wanting to become one.

Once I had all the information I needed, nobody could stop me, I took admission in B.Tech Honors Degree in Financial Engineering at Harare Institute of Technology (HIT). With this program, I developed a strong fond for quantitative-oriented courses like Mathematical and Statistical Methods in Finance, Stochastic Processes in Finance, Financial Econometrics, Options and Futures Markets, Financial Risk Management and Credit Derivatives. In my fourth year, I worked on a research project to investigate mis-valuation of stock prices at the Zimbabwe Stock Exchange. As part of the required capstone design, I developed an innovative Stock Valuation Index and a stock analysis web application using R Shine App.

With the skills and knowledge attained from my years at school and self-study, In 2017, I joined a community of quants around the world in developing and back-testing trading algorithms on QuantConnect, which is an open-source, cloud-based algorithmic trading platform for equities, FX, futures, options and cryptocurrencies.

This exposure left me with the hunger for more knowledge especially in the statistics field. I then took admission in Master’s Degree in Applied Statistics with Operational Research (MASOR) at University of Technology, Mauritius (UTM). I also got several certifications along the way, including Applied Data Science with Python offered by WorldQuant University. In 2019 I cofounded a company; QuanTech Africa and I worked as a lead Quantitative Researcher.

To date, working as a freelancer, I have had an opportunity to work with many individual traders, quants, trading teams and startups.
